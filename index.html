Shimano Groupsets

Shimano Road Groupsets Explained

Shimano has been producing high quality bicycle components since 1921 and is one of the leading component manufacturers along side SRAM and Campagnolo.

Dura-Ace


Dura-Ace is Shimano's top of the line components which is targeted at pro racers that go to take on the tour de france. These components have been designed to be as light as possible by being made of high quality carbon-fiber and titanium all to help keep the weight down. They have also been designed to be as aerodinamic as possible to help save on watts needed to go fast and keep top speed. These components are premium and typically stay over the thousand dollar mark because of all the features it has. The chain is actually hollow to save weight and is coated with premium material to keep chain wear down. The cassette also has special grooves to improove on shifting performance.


Ultegra

Shimano ultegra comes next to Dura-ace but is targeted at normal people. The components are nearly identical with the weight being heavier especially on the disc brakes that weight alot more than Dura-ace but also lack extra cooling fins needed to keep the temperature low to have consistent braking performance.


Shimano 105

"The groupset of the people" Shimano 105 is aimed at the avid cylist and enthusists who want a good high performance and quality groupset. Shimano 105 is on the cheaper side of their lineup which is why it is dubbed, "The groupset of the people" despite there being cheaper alternatives like, Tiagra, Sora and shimano Claris.
